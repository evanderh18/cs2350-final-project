Yellowstone

A national park that has a little bit of everything

About

Yellowstone is a national park that covers everyone's check list when it comes to exploring the outdoors. When you first drive into the caldera, the geological differences and activities make you think that you are on a different planet. However, there are several similarities when it comes to the overall vast forest landscape.

Location

Yellowstone is one of the largest national parks here in the United States. It is in fact so large this national park spreads across three states, Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho. With this park being so large, it is best to enjoy it over several days so you can see everything this park has to offer. Outside of the national park, there are a few towns outside you can enjoy at the end of the day. Specifically, Jackson Hole on the Wyoming side, and West Yellowstone on the Idaho side.

Geysers

Geysers are what Yellowstone is mostly known for. These unique geothermal activities will offer a very interesting highlight to your trip, specifically Old Faithful.

Hot Springs

The hot springs of Yellowstone are truly something worth seeing. With their hues of orange and blue, photos taken of these springs stand out. Just do not swim in them!

Wildlife

Yellowstone has a variety of wildlife. Due to the park being so large, and with all the animals being protected, you see a lot of wildlife compared to what you would be able to see in your local mountains. Not to mention that this is one of the few places in America where there are still wolves.
